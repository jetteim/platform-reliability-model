# Principles

## Reliability Is Socio-Technical

Reliability is produced by systems, people, process, tooling, product decisions, and organizational learning. Do not reduce it to uptime, infrastructure health, or alert volume.

## Incident Response And Aftercare Are Different

Incident response restores service and limits impact. Postmortem aftercare preserves evidence, explains contributing causes, generates learning, and creates improvement work.

## Blameless, Evidence-Backed Learning

Reliability analysis should explain what happened and why it made sense at the time. Avoid blame, vague lessons, or action items that do not follow from evidence.

## Actionability

Action items must improve one or more reliability capabilities:

- prevention
- detection
- first response
- dispatch
- repair
- automatic mitigation
- graceful degradation
- future learning

## Intent First

Ticket fields, workflow statuses, dashboards, chat channels, and backend APIs are implementation details. The model defines neutral intent objects that can be mapped to those tools.
